The High Speed Two (HS2) railway linking London, the Midlands and the North of England is much more than a transport project. It’s a demonstration of how the UK is building its future by reducing carbon emissions from transport and creating new opportunities for all through increased rail connectivity and capacity.

We know that stations can transform cities, and HS2 illustrates how transport networks can act as catalysts for sustainable regeneration and inclusive economic growth.
In 2020, the UK government gave instruction for main works construction of HS2 Phase One (London to Birmingham) to begin, allowing the pace of the programme to accelerate. Our contribution to shaping this transformative project began in 2009 and continues.

We are the lead environment and sustainability advisor for the full programme as well as a multidisciplinary consultant and designer for much of the route. We are also the architect, engineer and masterplanner for a number of HS2 station developments along the route.
